Social media guidelines & best practices.

A quick reference for all LatentView employees.

Protect.

Privacy and confidentiality come first — always.

01
Get consent before posting about anyone.
Don't

Do not post images or videos of other LatentView employees — real or AI-generated — without their explicit permission. When in doubt, don't post.

02
Never name clients or accounts.
Don't

Do not name client / account names anywhere — not in posts, captions, comments, images, or even job titles on your profile. This is a legal mandate.

Represent.

When you show the brand, show it right.

03
Follow the brand guidelines.
Do

Anything featuring LatentView's solutions, case studies, or offerings must align with our official brand guidelines — logo, colors, tone, and visual system.

04
Never AI-generate the logo.
Don't

If you use AI to design visuals, do not let it generate the LatentView logo. Always use the official logo.

05
Strictly avoid copyrighted media.
Don't

Don't combine LatentView communication with copyrighted music, video, images or third-party content. It puts the brand — and you — at legal risk.

Engage.

Show up like a thought leader.

06
Reshare — but add your voice.
Do

We love when you amplify LatentView content. Add your own perspective, takeaway, or context so the post lands better with your network and builds your personal brand.

07
Tag with intention.
Don't

Avoid excessively tagging LatentView leadership or colleagues without a clear reason. Tag when it adds context — not for reach.
